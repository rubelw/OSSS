## Definition of Bias
Bias is a systematic tendency to present information in a way that favors a particular perspective.

## Types of Bias
- Framing bias: presenting the same fact in a way that influences interpretation
- Omission bias: leaving out relevant context
- Selection bias: highlighting certain facts while ignoring others
- Language bias: using emotionally loaded or persuasive wording
- Authority bias: relying heavily on experts without counterpoints

## Indicators of Bias in School Board Materials
- Large financial changes without explanation
- Selective presentation of data trends
- Lack of alternative viewpoints
- Use of positive language for preferred outcomes and neutral/negative for others
- Repeated justification language

## Questions to Ask
- What is missing?
- What assumptions are being made?
- Who benefits from this framing?
- Is opposing data acknowledged?

## Output Format
When analyzing a document:
- Identify possible bias
- Provide evidence (quote text)
- Rate confidence: low / medium / high
- Avoid speculation without evidence
